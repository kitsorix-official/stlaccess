Why do these numbers matter?

In the world of 3D printing, "Scale" is often a marketing term rather than a physical measurement. Most fantasy miniatures are measured to the Eye Level, whereas historical kits use Head Height. STLAccess normalizes these systems using a universal 1.75m human baseline.

Scale Reference FAQ

Why are there two percentage columns (Eye Level vs Top of Head)?

Most fantasy miniatures are measured to the eyes (160mm on a 175cm person), while historical kits often use total height. Use "Eye Level" for character miniatures; "Top of Head" for vehicles or helmets.

Do I need to adjust for resin shrinkage?

Yes. Standard resin shrinks ~1-2%. For critical scale accuracy, add +1.5% to your slicer percentage (e.g., use 115.8% instead of 114.3% for 28mm→32mm).

How often are these conversion values updated?

Values are reviewed quarterly against official rulebooks and community consensus. Last updated: April 2026.
