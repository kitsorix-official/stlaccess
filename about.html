The Mission

Precision in a World of Scale Creep

How a 3D printing hobbyist turned mathematical frustration into a free tool for creators to use and learn from.

The Problem: "32mm" Means Nothing

If you've been in the 3D printing or tabletop hobby for more than a week, you've seen it. You download a beautiful "32mm" miniature, print it, and find out it towers over your "28mm" army like a giant. Or worse, it looks like a child standing next to a tank that was supposedly "1:56 scale."

In the industry, we call this Scale Creep. Every sculptor has their own definition of height. Some measure to the top of the head, others to the eye level. Some account for base thickness, others don't. For the hobbyist trying to build a cohesive collection, it's a nightmare of wasted resin and failed prints.

The Solution: The 175cm Baseline

STLAccess was born in a small bedroom workshop. As a 3D printing hobbyist, I realized that while "28mm" is a marketing term, "1:56" is a mathematical ratio. To bridge the gap, I needed a universal truth.

I established the 175cm Human Baseline. By assuming a standard human height of 1.75 meters, I can calculate exactly how large a model should be in any ratio or system scale.

Eye Level (160cm)

The standard for fantasy and sci-fi miniatures where heroic proportions often distort the "top of head" measurement.

Top of Head (175cm)

The gold standard for historical military kits and display-grade busts where realism is paramount.

From €40 to High-End Precision

My journey started in my bedroom with a tiny Easythreed printer from China—a €40 entry point into a world I didn't yet realize I'd be obsessed with. It was loud, it was temperamental, and it taught me everything about the importance of precision the hard way.

Fast forward to today, and my "workshop" has evolved. I now rely on a Bambu Lab X1 Carbon with a multi-color filament storage and feed system. This leap in technology—from a budget starter to a high-speed, multi-material powerhouse—is what drives the accuracy of STLAccess. I've seen the struggle of budget machines and the complexity of high-end ones, and I've built this tool to serve both.

Mathematical Certainty for Every Maker

My goal is to grow STLAccess, with the help of the 3D printing community, into the go-to resource for math conversions in tabletop games like Warhammer and more. My engine doesn't just guess; it computes based on our verified reference library. I provide the exact percentages you need to enter into your slicer—whether it's Lychee, Chitubox, or Bambu Studio—to ensure that every print fits perfectly within your world.

I believe that scale should be a choice, not a surprise. Precision is the standard, and at STLAccess, I'm making it accessible to everyone. For technical details on how I maintain global standards, view my compliance statement.
